## TL;DR — the decisions (locked)

1. **Engine:** Laravel **Cashier (Stripe)**. First-party Laravel package; handles the whole
   subscription lifecycle (trials, upgrades/downgrades, proration, cancellation, grace periods),
   webhooks, and invoice PDFs. We do not hand-roll subscription state.
2. **Subscriber = the `business`** (the tenant), NOT the user. The `Billable` trait goes on the
   `Business` model. A user who owns multiple businesses gets one subscription **per business**.
3. **Provider:** Stripe, **strictly B2B**, no consumer checkout, no product/service sales — only
   SaaS subscriptions. Tax is handled via reverse-charge + Stripe Tax (collect a tax ID; flip on
   Stripe Tax when volume warrants). Paddle/merchant-of-record was considered and rejected: with
   B2B reverse-charge the tax burden is minimal, and Stripe is lower-friction, lower-fee, and more
   flexible. Re-evaluate Paddle only if material international **consumer** volume ever appears
   (it won't, per the B2B-only decision).
4. **Stripe account:** use a **dedicated Balloonventory Stripe account**, separate from Todd's
   existing WordPress/CMS Stripe usage. Products, prices, webhooks, payouts, and reporting all live
   at the account level; separation keeps webhooks and reporting clean and makes the product easy to
   spin off later. One Stripe login can own multiple accounts.
5. **Scope of this effort:** Phase 1 (core billing) + Phase 2 (admin surfaces). **Coupons/discounts
   are IN scope** (native Stripe Promotion Codes via Cashier — required, they also back comps).
   **Referrals are deferred** and, when built, are an **in-house referral-credit program** —
   referrals earn **account credit** (free months / Stripe credit balance), **no cash, no payouts,
   no 1099, no third-party tool** — plus a small **VIP influencer coupon** class (1–3 partners)
   handled individually. The cash affiliate-platform path (FirstPromoter/Rewardful) is **ruled out**.
   See the **Coupons & Referrals** section.
6. **Swap-insurance:** all tier/feature checks go through OUR abstraction (`config/plans.php` +
   `Plan` enum + `PlanGate`), never Cashier directly, so a future provider swap stays a thin
   adapter change. The painful part of switching is migrating saved cards/subscriptions (can't move
   between processors) — cheapest while small, which argues against over-optimizing the choice now.

  environment, successor to "Test mode"). Sandbox data does **not** auto-copy to live — at launch,

- **Secrets handling:** Todd puts keys in the app's `.env` himself (`STRIPE_KEY`, `STRIPE_SECRET`,

## Comps & plan lifecycle

How entitlement, comps, plan removal, and billing intervals work. The unifying idea: **entitlement
lives in OUR system, not Stripe** — Stripe only charges cards. That makes comps and grandfathering
cheap.

### Effective-plan resolution (the one rule)

`PlanGate` resolves a business's effective plan in this order:

> **comp (if active) → Stripe-synced `businesses.plan` → Free**

Every gate/limit check uses the *effective* plan. This single rule covers all cases uniformly:
admins, testers, friends-and-family, and real paying customers all flow through it.

### Comps (free plans without Stripe)

- Add a comp layer to `businesses`: **`comped_plan`** (nullable enum, e.g. `pro`) + **`comped_until`**
  (nullable timestamp; NULL = forever). Set/cleared by an admin action.
- A comped business gets its `comped_plan` entitlement with **no Stripe subscription, no card, no
  charge** — `PlanGate` returns the comp before it ever looks at Stripe.
- **Admin/team businesses:** comp them to Pro/top tier (optionally auto-comp any business owned by a
  platform admin via `admin_level`, so Todd's own businesses never hit a paywall).
- **Alpha testers / friends:** comp Pro for a duration or indefinitely.
- This is the backing mechanism for the Phase 2 admin **"comp free months" / "reset trial"** actions.
- **Alternative (only if you want the comp visible in Stripe reporting):** a Stripe **100%-off
  coupon** — the business "subscribes" but pays $0. More moving parts than the no-Stripe grant;
  prefer the grant for internal/admin comps.

### Removing / retiring a plan

- **Stop offering it:** remove from the offered list in `config/plans.php` (disappears from the
  picker). Keep the tier *defined* so businesses still on it resolve correctly.
- **Stripe:** **archive** the Price (Stripe forbids deleting a Price with live subscriptions —
  archiving makes it non-purchasable while existing subs keep running). **Never hard-delete.**
- **Existing subscribers:** decide per removal — **grandfather** (keep charging the old price, keep
  entitlement) or **migrate** to a current tier (a Cashier `swap()` call). Both are simple.

### Billing intervals (monthly / quarterly / annual)

- An interval is just another **Stripe Price on the same Product** (`interval=month`,
  `interval_count=3` for quarterly; `interval=year` for annual). Each interval is a separate
  **Price ID** listed under its tier in `config/plans.php`.
- The customer billing page offers the available intervals as a toggle. Add or drop intervals
  anytime — no schema or code change, just config + a Stripe Price.

## Coupons & Referrals

Two related concerns: discounts (build now, native) and referral attribution (deferred, but design
the seams now). **Coupons are required no matter what** — they back launch promos, the comp
mechanism, AND the deferred referral/VIP work — so they are NOT deferred. **No cash affiliate
program / no third-party affiliate tool** — referral rewards are account credit (see below).

### Coupons & discounts — native Stripe, via Cashier (Phase 1/2, REQUIRED)

Stripe has two layers and Cashier speaks both:
- **Coupon** = the discount definition (percent or fixed; one-time / repeating N months / forever;
  restrictable to specific products).
- **Promotion Code** = the customer-facing code (e.g. `LAUNCH20`) mapping to a coupon, with
  guardrails: expiry, max redemptions, first-time-customer-only, minimum amount, per-customer caps.

Cashier support: `->withPromotionCode()` / `->withCoupon()` at subscribe,
`Subscription::applyPromotionCode()`, and read-back of the applied discount for display.

**Best practices / rules:**
- **Define and validate discounts in Stripe, never in our app.** Our billing page has a "promo
  code" field that passes the code to Cashier; Stripe does the math + proration. Do NOT build a
  discount engine.
- **Customer-facing → Promotion Codes** (shareable, restrictable, trackable). **Programmatic/admin
  → raw Coupons.**
- **Restrict every public code** (expiry + max redemptions + first-time-only + min amount) — that's
  the abuse guard.
- A **100%-off coupon** is the "comp via Stripe reporting" path (vs. the no-Stripe grant in
  **Comps & plan lifecycle** — prefer the grant for internal/admin comps).
- Surface the applied discount on the customer billing page.

**Where it lands:** Phase 1 = the promo-code field at checkout + apply/display. Phase 2 = admin
create/manage of coupons & promo codes (or just create them in the Stripe dashboard early on).

### Referrals & influencer rewards — in-house credit, NO cash (DEFERRED)

**Decision (Todd):** no cash affiliate program and **no third-party affiliate platform**
(FirstPromoter/Rewardful are **ruled out**). Referral rewards are **account credit**, which removes
the hardest parts of an affiliate program at once: no payouts, no payout fraud/multi-currency, and
**no 1099** (account credit toward your own service is generally a discount, not reportable income —
confirm with accountant). Removing cash is exactly what makes **in-house the right build** here.

Two mechanisms, both riding on the Phase 1/2 coupon + comp infrastructure:

**A. Referral-credit program (general — the default).**
- Each business gets a **referral code/link**; a referred signup is stamped
  `referred_by_business_id` (attribution).
- **Earn on conversion, not signup** — credit is granted only when the referred business **becomes
  a paying customer** (passes trial / first successful payment). This is the key anti-fraud rule.
- **Reward = account credit** accrued in a `referral_credits` ledger (earned / redeemed / balance).
  Default reward: **1 free month per converting referral** (a flat $ credit is an alternative —
  Todd's call).
- **Redemption** reuses what we already have: extend **`comped_until`** (free months) or apply a
  Stripe customer **credit balance** that auto-reduces the next invoice. The ledger is the source of
  truth; it just translates into one of those. **No cash ever leaves.**
- **Guardrails:** no self-referral; **cap or expire** banked credit to bound liability;
  **clawback-free by design** (granted only after conversion, so an immediate churn rarely costs
  you — skip refund-clawback logic).

**B. VIP influencer coupons (1–3 special partners).**
- A few influencers (e.g. a podcast host, a business coach who signs up their clients) get **special
  "VIP" Stripe Promotion Codes** — the same coupon machinery, flagged VIP for tracking. The code
  does double duty: gives their audience a discount AND lets Stripe count redemptions per influencer.
- Their reward is **handled individually/manually** — a negotiated arrangement ("something
  special"), not an automated program. If it's a cash payout, **record it in the Expense Ledger**
  (`prompts/expense_ledger_plan.md`, marketing/contractor category); if in-kind, grant extra
  credit/comp. It's a relationship, not a platform.

**Do-now seam (Phase 1, ~free):** standardize on **Promotion Codes** and let Cashier
customer-creation stamp **referral metadata** (`referred_by_business_id`) on the Stripe customer.
That's all that's needed now to make both A and B easy to add later. Nothing about referrals blocks
launch.

## Provider-agnostic abstraction (build first, it's the spine)

Everything the app checks goes through OUR code, never Cashier directly:

- **`config/plans.php`** — single source of truth: tier keys → Stripe price IDs (monthly/annual),
  limits, and feature flags. Driven by the Tier Structure stub above.
- **`App\Enums\Plan`** — typed tier enum; backs/replaces the `businesses.plan` string. TitleCase
  keys per PHP conventions.
- **`App\Support\PlanGate`** (or `Business::planAllows()` / `Business::planLimit()`) — answers
  *"can this business do X?"* and *"how many Y is it allowed?"*. The inventory app asks `PlanGate`;
  it never asks Stripe. This is the security/correctness core and gets the heaviest unit tests.

## Phase 3 — Referrals & influencer rewards (DEFERRED, in-house, NO cash)

In-house only — **no cash payouts, no 1099, no third-party affiliate platform** (FirstPromoter/
Rewardful ruled out). See the **Coupons & Referrals** section for the full design. Two mechanisms:

- **Referral-credit program (default):** per-business referral code → `referred_by_business_id`
